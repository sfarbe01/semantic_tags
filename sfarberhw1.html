Day One

The first stop on our trip was Niagara falls. Though there were a lot of tourists, we did have a yummy sandwich, got to see a rainbow, and of course, the falls. It was sort of like splash mountain, but for real. Any place that was good enough for Pam and Jim's wedding is pretty sacred to me!

Day Two

The next stop on our trip was Chicago. I was super pumped about revisiting the place where I was born. Not to mention getting to visit the Georges Seurat painting I slaved over for months. Well I'm not a painter, but I did put together a 3000 piece puzzle of his work! We were able to hit the Art Institute, the downtown area, and of course: Lou Malnatis.

Day Three

The next stop on our trip was Minneapolis. This was mostly a driving day, but we did eat at some awesome restaurants once we arrived. We also went to a run down mill which was pretty cool. Highlight was the sausage place we went for dinner which was described to me as a "hipster meat palace"

Day Four

From Minneapolis we headed to Rapid City, SD. We stopped off at the Badlands where we saw bison, antelope, and a pretty cool sunset.

Day Five

The next day we checked out Mt Rushmore before heading to Yellowstone. The place really rocked.

Life sucks in bear country.

Day Six

The next stop on our trip was Bozeman, MT. We stopped at this awesome place for dinner called Montana Ale Works. Was a super beautiful drive with lots of mountains.

Day Seven

The second to last day was by far my favorite. Glacier National Park was by far one of the most breathtaking places I have ever been.

Day Eight

Our final stop was Seattle! The land of breweries and the freshest coffee beans.
